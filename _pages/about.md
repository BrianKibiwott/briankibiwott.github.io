I am a pharmacist and clinical epidemiologist with experience in designing and analysing large-scale, register-based studies using routinely collected healthcare data. My research applies advanced epidemiological and statistical methods to investigate complex clinical and public health questions, with a particular focus on respiratory disease, pharmacoepidemiology, and other therapeutic areas.

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Gothenburg, collaborating with the Obstructive Lung Disease in Northern Sweden (OLIN) study at Umeå University. My work spans respiratory and cardiovascular epidemiology, infectious disease outcomes, and vaccine effectiveness and safety.

My methodological interests include causal inference, longitudinal data analysis, comparative effectiveness research, and machine learning for observational health data. My goal is to generate robust evidence that informs clinical practice, public health policy, and healthcare decision-making.

I welcome collaborations in epidemiology, pharmacoepidemiology, real-world evidence, and healthcare registry research.
