I am a pharmacist and clinical epidemiologist with experience in designing and analysing large-scale, register-based studies using routinely collected healthcare data. My research applies advanced epidemiological and statistical methods to investigate complex clinical and public health questions.

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher with the Obstructive Lung Disease in Northern Sweden (OLIN) study at Umeå University, collaborating with colleagues at the University of Gothenburg. My research spans respiratory and cardiovascular epidemiology, infectious diseases, and pharmacoepidemiology. In respiratory, cardiovascular, and infectious disease epidemiology, I investigate disease characteristics, clinical course, risk factors, and outcomes. In pharmacoepidemiology, I generate real-world evidence to evaluate the utilization, effectiveness, and safety of medicines and vaccines.

My methodological interests include causal inference, longitudinal data analysis, comparative effectiveness research, and machine learning for observational health data. My goal is to generate robust evidence that informs clinical practice, public health policy, and healthcare decision-making.

I welcome collaborations in epidemiology, pharmacoepidemiology, real-world evidence, and healthcare registry research.
